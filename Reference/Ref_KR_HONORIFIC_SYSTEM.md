## Overview

Korean Murim novels use a complex honorific system based on **sect hierarchy** (generation, rank, lineage) rather than age or social status. This system combines:

1. **Generational Seniority** (generations within sect)
2. **Rank-Based Titles** (sect leader, elder, disciple)
3. **External Relations** (sect-to-sect, warrior-to-warrior)
4. **Speech Level Formality** (존댓말 vs 반말)

**Key Difference from Japanese:**
- Japanese: Age + social status based (senpai/kouhai by entry year)
- Korean Murim: Generation-based (사형/사제 by master lineage)

## Sect Hierarchy Generations

### The Generational System (항렬/行列)

Murim sects use **generation names** (항렬자) to mark lineage depth:

```
Founder → 1st Gen → 2nd Gen → 3rd Gen → 4th Gen...
           (현)      (운)      (검)      (무)
         Hyun-    Un-      Geom-    Mu-
```

**Example: Mount Hua Sect (화산파)**
- 1st Generation: 현 (Hyun-) → Hyun Jong, Hyun Sang, Hyun Young
- 2nd Generation: 운 (Un-) → Un Gum, Un Hye, Un Am
- 3rd Generation: 검 (Geom-) → None in current timeline
- Disciple names: Chung Myung (청명), Yoon Jong (윤종), Jo Gul (조걸)

**Hierarchy Rule:**
- Same generation = 사형/사제 (senior/junior by entrance order)
- Different generations = 사숙/사질 (uncle/nephew structure)

### Five Generation Depth

Most sects maintain 3-5 active generations:

| Generation | Title | Korean | Hán Việt | Role |
|-----------|-------|--------|----------|------|
| Founder | 개조/창시자 | 開祖/創始者 | Khai tổ | Sect founder (usually deceased) |
| Grand Elder | 태상장로 | 太上長老 | Thái Thượng Trưởng Lão | Retired leaders |
| Elder | 장로 | 長老 | Trưởng lão | Senior masters |
| Disciple | 제자 | 弟子 | Đệ tử | Main body |
| Trainee | 수습/신입 | 修習/新入 | Tập sự | Newcomers |

## Internal Sect Honorifics

### Same Generation Address (동세대/同世代)

**Basic Structure:** [Name] + 사형/사저/사제/사매

| Korean | Hanja | Gender | Status | Hán Việt | Usage Example |
|--------|-------|--------|--------|----------|---------------|
| 사형 | 師兄 | Male | Senior | Sư huynh | "청명 사형" (Chung Myung sahyeong) |
| 사저 | 師姐 | Female | Senior | Sư tỷ | "소소 사저" (Soso sajeo) |
| 사제 | 師弟 | Male | Junior | Sư đệ | "윤종 사제" (Yoon Jong saje) |
| 사매 | 師妹 | Female | Junior | Sư muội | "당소소 사매" (Tang Soso samae) |

**Special Titles:**

| Korean | Hanja | Meaning | Hán Việt | Role |
|--------|-------|---------|----------|------|
| 대사형 | 大師兄 | Great Senior Brother | Đại Sư Huynh | Eldest male of generation |
| 대사저 | 大師姐 | Great Senior Sister | Đại Sư Tỷ | Eldest female of generation |
| 수제자 | 首弟子 | Chief Disciple | Thủ Đệ Tử | Successor candidate |

**Speech Patterns:**
- **To 사형/사저 (senior):** MUST use 존댓말 (formal speech) + honorific suffix
  - "사형, 가르침을 주시겠습니까?" (Sahyeong, will you teach me?)
- **To 사제/사매 (junior):** CAN use 반말 (informal) or 존댓말 (contextual)
  - "사제, 수련을 열심히 해라." (Saje, train diligently.)
  - "사제, 수련 잘 하고 있어?" (Saje, training well?)

### Cross-Generation Address (세대간)

**One Generation Above:**

| Korean | Hanja | Gender | Hán Việt | Meaning |
|--------|-------|--------|----------|---------|
| 사숙 | 師叔 | Male | Sư thúc | Martial uncle |
| 사고 | 師姑 | Female | Sư cô | Martial aunt |
| 사백 | 師伯 | Male (elder) | Sư bá | Elder martial uncle |

**One Generation Below:**
- 사질 (師姪) - Martial nephew/niece (Sư chất)

**Two+ Generations Above:**
- 사숙조 (師叔祖) - Great martial uncle (Sư Thúc Tổ)
- 사태 (師太) - Grand martial aunt (Sư Thái)

**Speech Patterns:**
- **To generation above:** ALWAYS 존댓말 + respectful tone
  - "사숙, 제자가 여쭙겠습니다." (Sasuk, this disciple asks...)
- **To generation below:** 반말 acceptable, but kind tone
  - "사질, 잘 지내고 있느냐?" (Sajil, are you doing well?)

## Speech Level System (존댓말/반말)

### Korean Formality Hierarchy

| Level | Korean | Usage | Verb Ending Example |
|-------|--------|-------|---------------------|
| Formal High | 합쇼체 | Very formal, distant | -습니다/-ㅂ니다 |
| Formal Mid | 해요체 | Polite, common | -아요/-어요 |
| Informal Mid | 하오체 | Old formal (historical) | -소/-오 |
| Informal Low | 해라체 | Casual, to juniors | -다/-라/-냐 |

**Murim Context:**

| Relationship | Speech Level | Example | Translation |
|-------------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| To Sect Leader | 합쇼체 (highest) | "배알합니다" | "I pay my respects" |
| To Senior (사형) | 해요체 (polite) | "가르쳐 주세요" | "Please teach me" |
| To Junior (사제) | 해라체 (casual) | "잘 지내냐?" | "Are you doing well?" |
| To Equal (same rank) | 해요체 or 해라체 | Context-dependent | Varies |
| To Enemy | 해라체 or 하오체 | Formal-cold or casual-hostile | Varies |

### Contextual Speech Shifts

**Combat Situations:**
- Even to seniors: May drop to 해라체 in urgent combat
- "사형, 조심해!" (Sahyeong, watch out!) - casual but not disrespectful

**Emotional Moments:**
- High emotion → formality may drop
- "청명아, 제발!" (Chung Myung-a, please!) - intimate suffix -아

**Sect Meetings:**
- Formal settings → always 해요체 or higher
- "제자들이 모두 모였습니다." (All disciples have gathered.)

## Decision Tree for Honorific Selection

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Is speaker addressing someone?      │
└──┬──────────────────────────────────┘
   │
   ├─YES→ Same sect?
   │       ├─YES→ Same generation?
   │       │       ├─YES→ Who entered first?
   │       │       │       ├─Other entered first → Use 사형/사저 + 존댓말
   │       │       │       └─Self entered first → Use 사제/사매 + 반말/존댓말
   │       │       └─NO→ Who is senior generation?
   │       │               ├─Other is senior → Use 사숙/사고 + 존댓말
   │       │               └─Self is senior → Use 사질 + 반말
   │       └─NO→ What is their role?
   │               ├─Sect Leader (other sect) → "OO장문" + 존댓말
   │               ├─Monk/Taoist → 화상/도장 + 존댓말
   │               ├─Young warrior → 소협 + 해요체
   │               └─Famous warrior → 대협 + 존댓말
   │
   └─NO→ Narration/Internal thought
           └─Use Vietnamese pronouns (anh/chị/họ)
```

## Context Rules & Examples

### Rule 1: Entrance Order Matters (입문 순서)

Within the same generation, **who entered the sect first** determines seniority.

**Example:**
- Un Gum entered before Un Hye → Un Gum is 사형 to Un Hye
- Un Hye must call Un Gum "사형" and use 존댓말
- Un Gum can call Un Hye "사제" and use 반말 (but often chooses 존댓말 for politeness)

### Rule 2: Sect Leader Commands Absolute Respect

**Even to senior generation:**
- 장문인 (Sect Leader) > 장로 (Elders)
- Elders address Sect Leader with "장문인님" + extreme formality

**Example:**
```korean
Elder: "장문인님, 제가 건의드리고자 합니다."
(Jangmunin-nim, I wish to make a suggestion.)
```

### Rule 3: External Politeness (Sect Reputation)

When meeting other sects, **always be polite** to maintain sect honor.

**Even to enemies:**
- Use formal titles until hostilities begin
- "사파의 채주님, 무슨 일로 이곳에?" (Evil Sect Stronghold Leader, what brings you here?)

### Rule 4: Combat Drops Formality

**In battle:**
- Honorifics remain, but speech drops to 해라체
- Urgency trumps politeness

**Example:**
```korean
Normal: "사형, 뒤를 조심하세요!" (Sahyeong, please watch your back!)
Combat: "사형, 뒤다!" (Sahyeong, behind you!)
```

### Rule 5: Teacher-Student Bond Transcends Generation

**사부 (Sư phụ) relationship:**
- Master's students → lifetime respect, regardless of later rank
- Even if student becomes Sect Leader, respects original master

**Example:**
- Chung Myung respects his past-life master, though now both are "equal" in age

## Common Honorific Patterns

### Pattern 1: Seeking Permission

**Structure:** [Title] + [request verb] + 겠습니까?

```korean
"사형, 가르침을 주시겠습니까?" (Sahyeong, will you teach me?)
"장문인님, 출타를 허락해 주시겠습니까?" (Jangmunin-nim, will you permit me to leave?)
"사숙, 질문을 드려도 되겠습니까?" (Sasuk, may I ask a question?)
```

### Pattern 2: Reporting to Superiors

**Structure:** [Title] + [humble self-reference] + [report]

```korean
"장문인님, 제자가 돌아왔습니다." (Jangmunin-nim, this disciple has returned.)
"사형, 소제가 임무를 완료했습니다." (Sahyeong, this junior has completed the mission.)
"장로님, 문도가 보고드립니다." (Jangno-nim, this disciple reports.)
```

### Pattern 3: Giving Orders (Senior → Junior)

**Structure:** [Title] + [command verb] + 라/거라

```korean
"사제, 이것을 가져가거라." (Saje, take this.)
"사질, 수련장으로 가라." (Sajil, go to the training ground.)
"제자들, 집합하라!" (Disciples, assemble!)
```

### Pattern 4: Casual Between Equals

**Structure:** [Name] + suffix + casual speech

```korean
"윤종아, 잘 지내냐?" (Yoon Jong-a, are you well?)
"청명이, 밥 먹었어?" (Chung Myung-i, did you eat?)
"형, 오늘 같이 수련할래?" (Hyeong, want to train together today?)
```

### Pattern 5: Formal Introductions

**Structure:** [Sect name] + [generation/title] + [name] + 입니다

```korean
"화산파 삼대제자 청명입니다." (I am Chung Myung, third-generation disciple of Mount Hua.)
"무당파 장로 송원입니다." (I am Elder Song Won of Wudang Sect.)
"소림사 현 세대 법륜입니다." (I am Beob-ryun of the current Shaolin generation.)
```

## Regional & Sect Variations

### Shaolin Temple (Buddhist)

**Unique Terms:**
- 사형 → 사형 (same, but with Buddhist naming: 법륜, 법허)
- Master → 방장 (方丈 - Abbot)
- Monks address each other: "사형" but with Buddhist titles

**Speech:**
- More formal, compassionate tone
- "아미타불" (Amitabha) - Buddhist greeting

### Wudang Sect (Taoist)

**Unique Terms:**
- 사형 → 도형 (道兄 - Dao Brother) sometimes
- Master → 장문 or 도장
- Emphasis on "도우" (道友 - Dao Friend) for equals

**Speech:**
- Taoist philosophical language
- "무량천존" (Immeasurable Celestial Worthy) - Taoist greeting

### Demonic Cult (마교)

**Unique Terms:**
- 교주 (敎主 - Cult Leader) - supreme authority
- 호법 (護法 - Protector) - elite guards
- Less formal, more brutal speech

**Speech:**
- Casual or commanding tone
- "마교만세!" (Long live the Demonic Cult!)

### Beggars Sect (개방)

**Unique Terms:**
- 방주 (幇主 - Union Leader)
- 장로회 (Elder Council)
- Bags (자루) system: 9-bag elders > 8-bag members

**Speech:**
- More casual, street-smart language
- "형님" (Hyeong-nim - Big Brother) common

## Translation Guidelines for Vietnamese

### Keep Korean Terms in Dialogue

**Honorifics to KEEP in Vietnamese translation:**
- 사형/사저/사제/사매 → Keep as "Sahyeong/Sajeo/Saje/Samae"
- 장문인 → "Jangmunin" (or translate as "Chưởng môn")
- 도장 → "Dojang" (or "Đạo trưởng")
- 대협/소협 → "Daehyeop/Sohyeop" (or "Đại hiệp/Tiểu hiệp")

**Rationale:**
- Preserves cultural flavor
- Avoids awkward Vietnamese equivalents
- Readers recognize Murim terminology

### When to Translate

**Translate in narration:**
- 사형 → "người sư huynh" (the senior martial brother)
- 장문인 → "chưởng môn của phái" (the sect leader)
- 장로 → "các trưởng lão" (the elders)

**Example:**
```vietnamese
Dialogue: "Sahyeong, em cần sự giúp đỡ!"
Narration: Người sư huynh của cô gật đầu.
```

### Hybrid Approach

**First mention:** Introduce with Korean + Vietnamese

```vietnamese
"Jangmunin (chưởng môn), đệ tử xin hầu."
```

**Later mentions:** Use Korean term alone

```vietnamese
"Jangmunin gật đầu."
```
